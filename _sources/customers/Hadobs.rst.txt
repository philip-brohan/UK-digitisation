Met Office Global Datasets
==========================

The Met Office maintain many important, observation-based datasets that cover the whole world, not just the UK. Many distributed via `https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/ <https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/>`_.

.. figure:: https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/hadcrut4/data/current/web_figures/anomalies.png
   :width: 95%
   :align: center

   The HadCRUT4 global dataset (`Source <https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/hadcrut4/>`_). Dataset coverage, and accuracy, depend on having observations.

The upper-air and sub-surface ocean datasets are out of scope here, the others fall mostly into two categories:

* Marine datasets (e.g. `HadSST <https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/hadsst3/>`_, `HadISST <https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/hadisst/>`_) using the data in `ICOADS <http://icoads.noaa.gov>`_.
* Gridded land temperatures (e.g. `HadEX2 <https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/hadex2/>`_, `HadGHCND <https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/hadghcnd/>`_) using :doc:`international datasets derived from observations <Databases>`.

Maintaining and improving these datasets provides a strong justification for additional digitisation of :doc:`ship observations <../sources/ships>`, as well as support for :doc:`international datasets development <Databases>`.
